<p><p>The neonatal intensive care respiratory devices market includes several critical tools for managing respiratory challenges in newborns. Nebulizers deliver medication directly to the lungs, while inhalers provide aerosolized drugs for respiratory relief. Ventilators assist breathing by mechanically delivering air to infants who cannot breathe independently. Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP) devices maintain open airways to improve oxygenation. Apnea monitors track infants' breathing patterns to detect pauses, ensuring timely intervention and enhancing neonatal care during vulnerable stages of development.</p></p>
