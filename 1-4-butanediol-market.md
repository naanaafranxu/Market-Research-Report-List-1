<p><p>1,4-Butanediol (BDO) is produced through various industrial processes, each with distinct market implications. The Reppe process utilizes acetylene and formaldehyde, while the Davy process involves hydrogenation of succinic acid. The Butadiene process derives BDO from butadiene, and the Propylene oxide process uses propylene oxide as a precursor. Each method affects production costs, sustainability, and product purity, influencing the overall market dynamics and supply chain strategies for BDO, which is essential for plastics, solvents, and elastomers.</p></p>
