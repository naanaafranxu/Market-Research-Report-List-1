<p><ul><li>Diamine</li><li>Ternary Amine</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Amine salts are classified primarily into two types: diamine and tertiary amines. Diamine salts consist of two amine functional groups, enhancing their reactivity and application potential in pharmaceuticals and polymers. Tertiary amines, containing three organic groups attached to the nitrogen atom, are versatile and often used in surfactants, solvents, and catalysts. Both types of amine salts play crucial roles in various industrial processes, contributing to the production of specialty chemicals and improving performance in formulations across diverse applications.</p></p>
